# Doctrine (Reference)

Simon Wardley's **40 doctrine principles** — universal principles to apply **regardless of the map**. Contrast with gameplays, which are context-dependent.

Source: Simon Wardley's [Medium book](https://medium.com/wardleymaps). Historical note: his [2016 blog post](https://blog.gardeviance.org/2016/05/wardleys-doctrine.html) had only ~15 principles; the 40-principle / 4-phase form is the mature version.

Use this list when the skill's strategic-analysis step flags doctrine violations (e.g., missing user, unclear anchor, under-specified Knowledge layer).

## Notes

- **"Think small" appears twice on purpose.** #9 (Phase I, Operations, "know the details") and #20 (Phase II, Structure, "two pizza") are distinct.
- **FIRE, not FIST.** Wardley updated the acronym from FIST (simple, tiny) to FIRE (restrained, elegant).
- **Phase placement ambiguities.** "Be transparent" (Phase I vs II) and "Think big" (Phase III vs IV) have varied across Wardley's versions. The placement above reflects the current canonical form.
- **Doctrine is cumulative.** A Phase III organisation still applies Phases I and II.
- **Not everything is in the math model.** Principles #19, #20, #28, #30, #34, #35 are organisational; the math model cannot fully encode them.
