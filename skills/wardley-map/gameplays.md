# Gameplay Catalogue (Reference)

Simon Wardley's **61 gameplays** in **12 categories** — source: [*On 61 different forms of gameplay*, 2015](https://blog.gardeviance.org/2015/05/on-61-different-forms-of-gameplay.html).

Use this list when the skill's strategic-analysis step recommends plays: cite the named Wardley gameplay, not a generic suggestion.

## 1. Basic Operations (6)

1. **Focus on user needs** — Focus the organisation on user needs rather than internal needs.
2. **Situational Awareness** — Remove alignment issues between business, IT and other groups.
3. **Effective & Efficient** — Removal of bias and duplication within an organisation.
4. **Structure & Culture** — Implementation of cell-based & PST (Pioneer-Settler-Town Planner) structures.
5. **Optimising Flow** — Risk, performance, information, and financial flow analysed.
6. **Channel Conflict** — Exploiting new channels and conflict within existing channels.

## 2. User Perception (7)

7. **Education** — Overcoming user inertia to change through education.
8. **Bundling** — Hiding a disadvantageous change by bundling it with desired ones.
9. **Creating artificial needs** — Elevating an artificial need through marketing.
10. **Confusion of Choice** — Preventing rational decisions by overwhelming users.
11. **FUD** — Creating fear, uncertainty, doubt over a change.
12. **Artificial competition** — Creating two competing bodies as the focus of competition.
13. **Lobbying** — Persuading government of a favourable position.

## 3. Accelerators (5)

14. **Market Enablement** — Encouraging the development of competition in a market.
15. **Open Approaches** — Encouraging competition through open source, open data, open APIs.
16. **Exploiting Network Effects** — Marginal value increases with users.
17. **Co-operation** — Working with others.
18. **Industrial Policy** — Government investment in a field.

## 4. Deaccelerators (4)

(Wardley's spelling — not "Decelerators".)

19. **Exploiting existing constraints** — Finding and reinforcing a constraint.
20. **Patents & IPR** — Preventing competitors from developing a space.
21. **Creating constraints** — Supply chain manipulation to create constraint.
22. **Limitation of competition** — Through regulatory or other means.

## 5. Dealing with Toxicity (3)

23. **Disposal of liability** — Overcoming internal inertia to divest.
24. **Sweat & Dump** — A 3rd party takes over operating the toxic asset.
25. **Pig in a poke** — Creating the appearance that the toxic asset has value.

## 6. Market (6)

26. **Differentiation** — Creating a visible difference through user needs.
27. **Pricing policy** — Exploiting supply and demand effects.
28. **Exploiting buyer / supplier power** — Position of strength in the chain.
29. **Harvesting** — Let others develop on your offerings; buy winners.
30. **Standards game** — Driving a market to your standard.
31. **Signal distortion** — Manipulating commonly used market signals.

## 7. Defensive (4)

32. **Threat acquisition** — Buying companies that may threaten your market.
33. **Raising barriers to entry** — Increasing expectations within a market.
34. **Procrastination** — Do nothing and allow competition to drive a system.
35. **Defensive regulation** — Using government to protect your market.

## 8. Attacking (5)

36. **Directed investment** — VC approach to a specific or identified future change.
37. **Experimentation** — Specialist groups, hackdays.
38. **Creating centres of gravity** — Talent hubs that pull from rivals.
39. **Undermining barriers to entry** — Identifying and reducing a market barrier.
40. **Fool's mate** — Using a constraint to force industrialisation.

## 9. Ecosystem (7)

41. **Alliances** — Working with other companies to drive evolution.
42. **Co-creation** — Working with end users to drive evolution.
43. **Sensing Engines (ILC)** — Innovate-Leverage-Commoditise; use consumption data to detect future success.
44. **Tower and Moat** — Dominate a future position; prevent future competitors.
45. **Two factor** — Bringing consumers and producers together.
46. **Co-opting** — Copy competitor's move; undermine their ecosystem advantage.
47. **Embrace & Extend** — Capture an existing ecosystem.

## 10. Competitor (7)

48. **Tech Drops** — "Follow me" situations; drop large technology changes on rivals.
49. **Fragmentation** — Break up a rival's market via pricing / constraints.
50. **Reinforcing inertia** — Identify and amplify a rival's own inertia.
51. **Sapping** — Open multiple competitive fronts.
52. **Misdirection** — Send false signals to competitors.
53. **Restriction** — Limit a competitor's ability to adapt.
54. **Talent Raid** — Remove core talent from a competitor.

## 11. Positional (4)

55. **Land grab** — Position to capture a future market space.
56. **First mover** — Exploit first-mover advantage, especially with industrialisation.
57. **Fast follower** — Exploit fast-follower advantage into uncharted spaces.
58. **Weak Signal** — Use economic patterns to identify where and when to act.

## 12. Poison (3)

59. **Licensing** — Licensing regimes that prevent future competitor moves.
60. **Insertion** — Encourage rival to pursue false leads.
61. **Design to fail** — Remove future threats by poisoning a market space.

---

**Totals:** 6 + 7 + 5 + 4 + 3 + 6 + 4 + 5 + 7 + 7 + 4 + 3 = **61** ✓

**Not inertia:** FUD (#11), Lobbying (#13), Bundling (#8) are **gameplays** — don't confuse them with the 17 inertia forms (`inertia.md`).
