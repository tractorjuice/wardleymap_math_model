# Wardley Map: Gender as a Cultural Construct

**Anchor:** The individual person (Identity) — the emergent outcome of everything below.

**Date:** 2022-03 (scenario as-of)

**Scope:** A capability map of how gender is produced and reproduced as a cultural construct, running from the user-visible personal dimensions (identity, phenotype, sexual identity, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, lived experience) through the codification grammar (memory, stories, symbols, roles) and the normative layer (values, rights & beliefs), down to the collective structures (family, corporation, nation, patriarchy, matriarchy), the power-concentration machinery (hierarchies of authority, power, social class, property & ownership), and the biological substrate (epigenetics, genetic markers, environment).

## Step 2 — Value Chain

Working downward from the anchor (the individual person):

1. **Anchor — Identity.** The emergent outcome: a person's sense of self.
2. **Personal / lived-experience layer.** The user-visible expressive dimensions: Sexual Identity, Sexual Orientation, Gender Identity, Gender Expression, Lived Experience, Self-Expression, and where Exclusion bites.
3. **Phenotype.** The observable biological surface that the four sexual/gender dimensions and identity ride on.
4. **Codification layer.** How lived experience is turned into transmissible patterns: Memory → Stories → Symbols → Roles.
5. **Values and rights layer.** Values and Rights & Beliefs — the normative content that roles invoke.
6. **Collective structures.** Family, Corporation, Nation, Patriarchy, Matriarchy — the institutions that author and perpetuate roles and values.
7. **Power concentration layer.** Hierarchies of Authority, Power, Social Class, Property & Ownership — the industrialised social machinery where power concentrates.
8. **Biological substrate.** Epigenetics, Genetic Markers, Environment — the hidden raw material below phenotype.

Key dependency insight: **collectives sit below the roles they produce** — Roles, Values and Rights & Beliefs depend on Family, Nation, Corporation, Patriarchy, Matriarchy, not the other way around. Similarly, **collectives themselves rest on Property & Ownership, Power, Hierarchies of Authority** — the power-concentration machinery is the substrate of the substrate.

## Step 5 — Analysis

### Completeness
- Anchor (Identity) is clearly defined at the top.
- All components requested by the user are present: identity; phenotype; sexual identity; sexual orientation; gender identity; gender expression; lived experience; memory, stories, symbols, roles; family, nation, patriarchy, matriarchy, corporation; values; rights & beliefs; hierarchies of authority, power; social class; property & ownership; exclusion and self-expression; genetic markers, epigenetics, environment.
- Dependencies are stated explicitly in the `.owm` file (every `a->b` satisfies `visibility(a) >= visibility(b)`).
- Movement arrows are present (`evolve` declarations in the `.owm`).

### Positioning
- Commodity components (Patriarchy, Property & Ownership, Hierarchies of Authority, Roles, Symbols, Family, Nation) are on the right — they are the industrialised social machinery.
- Genuinely emergent components (Identity, Gender Identity, Lived Experience, Self-Expression, Matriarchy, Epigenetics) sit left or centre.
- Positioning is by **market/cultural maturity**, not by how "old" a concept is — Patriarchy is ancient but also highly industrialised and therefore commodity; Matriarchy is also an ancient concept but is still custom because it is rarely realised as societal infrastructure.

### Insights
- **Where power concentrates:** the bottom-right quadrant. Property & Ownership, Patriarchy, Hierarchies of Authority, Power, Social Class, Roles — all at commodity stage with strong inertia, all supporting the collectives above.
- **Individual vs. industrialised:** the diagonal divide is clear. Personal dimensions (top-centre: Gender Identity, Gender Expression, Lived Experience, Self-Expression) are custom/product. Industrialised machinery (bottom-right: Patriarchy, Property, Hierarchies) is commodity.
- **Acceleration in 2022:** the four personal dimensions (Gender Identity, Gender Expression, Sexual Identity, Sexual Orientation) and Rights & Beliefs are moving fast — they are the locus of current cultural change.
- **Inertia points:** Patriarchy, Hierarchies of Authority, Property & Ownership, and Nation are marked inertia. Their evolution rightward is structural, not technical; they resist being repositioned even when the personal layer above them has already moved.
- **Build-vs-buy analogue:** a movement from Patriarchy toward Matriarchy (or either-or) is a **genesis-stage bet** (0.32 on the evolution axis). Most institutional work in 2022 is happening in the **Rights and Beliefs / Exclusion** layer — productising anti-discrimination frameworks — not at the power-concentration layer below it.

### Strategic patterns (climate / gameplay lens)
- **Peace / War / Wonder:** the **personal/expressive layer is in a "wonder" period** (rapid change, new vocabularies, legal experimentation), while the **power-concentration layer is in "peace"** (stable, entrenched, commoditised).
- **Inertia forms:** institutional inertia at Patriarchy, Nation; infrastructural inertia at Property & Ownership; cultural inertia at Roles and Symbols (changing symbols is slow even when values move fast).
- **Commoditisation from above:** Rights & Beliefs is being pushed from Product toward Commodity by supranational frameworks (EU, UN) — a classic "component above a commodity is itself commoditising" pattern.
- **Doctrine weakness:** most public debate focuses on the **personal layer** (where change is visible) rather than the **power-concentration layer** (where change would be durable). This is a mis-targeting of strategic effort.
