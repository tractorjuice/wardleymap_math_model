# Wardley Map — Regenerative Farming Landscape (August 2022)

## Scope and framing

This map treats *how food is produced* as the underlying value chain and deliberately uses **six anchors** because regenerative farming is not a single user-need: consumers buy food, farmers make agronomic decisions, retailers set procurement rules, national governments run subsidies, supranational bodies (UN/FAO/IPCC) set treaty-level direction, and certification bodies operate the trust layer that connects practice to price. Collapsing any of these into a single anchor hides exactly the interesting structure the scenario asks about — namely, where practice is knowledge-bound, where industrial practice has locked in, and where supply-chain awareness is thin.

The timepoint is August 2022, roughly:

- **post-UNFSS** (UN Food Systems Summit Sept 2021), still in follow-up phase — no binding output
- **pre-COP27** (Sharm-El-Sheikh, Nov 2022) — Koronivia Joint Work on Agriculture is live but soft
- **post-Ukraine-invasion** (Feb 2022) — synthetic N fertiliser prices at record highs, creating a short-term economic incentive to cut inputs that briefly aligns with the regenerative case
- **US IRA** signed August 2022 — ~USD 20bn earmarked for climate-smart agriculture, MRV still unresolved
- **UK ELM / ELMS** in pilot, not yet at rollout
- **EU CAP post-2023 reform** agreed but not in force — eco-schemes on paper only

## Value chain summary

Working from the anchor downward, the map groups components into eight bands:

1. **Consumer-facing signals** — labels, dietary choice, trust
2. **Retail / brand** — sustainability commitments, Scope 3 disclosure, private-label regen ranges
3. **Policy and treaty** — CAP/Farm Bill (commodity/entrenched) vs. ELM/IRA soil-carbon payments (still custom) vs. UNFCCC agricultural workstreams (still directional)
4. **Measurement / MRV (monitoring, reporting, verification)** — the connective tissue between farming practice and market signals
5. **Farming practice** — the regenerative bundle (rotation, cover crops, no-till, managed grazing, agroforestry) vs. the extractive/industrial bundle (monoculture, synthetic inputs)
6. **Knowledge / advisory** — conventional agronomy vs. regen peer networks and extension
7. **Energy matrix beneath the soil** — soil microbiome, mycorrhizal networks, photosynthesis-to-exudate pathway vs. Haber-Bosch nitrogen, phosphate, synthetic pesticides
8. **Substrate and conservation** — land, water, pollinators, climate, biodiversity set-asides
9. **Supply-chain awareness infrastructure** — satellite imagery, cloud platforms, IoT/traceability pilots

## Where things sit — the interesting placements

**Knowledge-bound (Genesis / early Custom, 0.05-0.30):**

- *Soil microbiome science* (0.28) and the *photosynthesis → root-exudate pathway* (0.24). In Aug 2022 this is still research-lab territory — the causal chain from plant sugars to rhizosphere microbial communities to soil carbon stability is understood in outline but nothing like the standard operating procedure of Haber-Bosch.
- *Mycorrhizal / fungal networks (applied)* (0.26). Known since the 1990s but reliable in-field inoculation is still patchy and poorly standardised.
- *On-farm biodiversity monitoring* (0.54) and *carbon labelling* (0.78) sit high on the visibility axis but far left on evolution — consumers see these but the methods underneath are immature.
- *Scope 3 supply-chain disclosure* (0.68) is visible at retailer-board level but the actual per-SKU accounting is genesis-to-custom.

**Locked in as commodity (0.85+):**

- *Synthetic N fertiliser* (0.96), *phosphate/K* (0.90), *synthetic pesticides* (0.95) — fully industrial, global commodity supply chain, priced on oil/gas feedstock.
- *Monoculture cropping* (0.95) — the default in every major cereals belt.
- *Commodity seed varieties* (0.88) — F1 hybrids, proprietary, supply-chain-locked.
- *National subsidy regimes* (CAP / US Farm Bill) (0.92) — commodity as a political artifact: the incumbent policy *is* the utility, and every farmer plans around it.
- *Arable land, water, climate* — substrate commodities, but increasingly priced-at-risk by climate change.

**The bridge zone (0.40-0.70, Custom → Product):**

This is where the map is most kinetic. *MRV standards* (VCS / Gold Std / ISO 14064) are moving from custom to product as the US IRA forces them to stabilise; *farm-level GHG accounting* and *soil organic carbon measurement* are right behind. *Regenerative certification schemes* (ROC, Regenerative Organic, RA) are clearly in the "many competing approaches, no clear winner" zone — classic Custom-Built.

## Supply-chain awareness picture

The user asked specifically. In August 2022:

- **Upstream from farm**: awareness is decent — fertiliser and chemical inputs have well-established supply chains with SAP-style tracking. The shock is cost (Ukraine), not visibility.
- **Farm → first buyer (co-op / grain elevator / processor)**: patchy. Traceability resets at every handoff.
- **Processor → brand → retailer**: label-driven. Organic and Fair Trade have certified chain-of-custody. Regenerative claims mostly do not.
- **Scope 3 disclosure**: retailer-side pressure is real (Walmart, Tesco, Nestlé, Unilever all have 2030 commitments), but the per-field-level emissions number that would actually attach to a bag of flour is a custom-engineering effort in every case. This is why the map places *Scope 3 disclosure* at the product-stage on retailer **visibility** but at only 0.22 on evolution — the demand is concrete, the fulfilment is bespoke.

Net: supply-chain awareness is being built top-down (retailer Scope 3) and bottom-up (satellite imagery, MRV standards) and the two sides have not met in the middle.

## Movement to expect

Evolution arrows on the map reflect where I expect component positions to sit ~2025-2030, assuming no exogenous shock greater than Ukraine-2022:

- *MRV Standard* (0.52 → 0.68) — IRA and ELM will force convergence.
- *No-Till / Reduced Tillage* (0.55 → 0.72) — already near product in North America, will tip to commodity in row crops.
- *Cover Cropping* (0.38 → 0.58) — moves from custom to product.
- *Regen Certification Scheme* (0.50 → 0.58) — one or two schemes consolidate.
- *Carbon labelling* (0.18 → 0.48) — large retailers force an interoperable label.
- *Soil microbiome science* (0.28 → 0.35) — slow; this remains largely knowledge-bound through 2030.

## Inertia

Cross-marked components on the map:

| Component | Why it resists |
|---|---|
| Synthetic N fertiliser | 110 years of Haber-Bosch industrial infrastructure; priced on gas; job base in 50+ countries |
| Synthetic pesticide/herbicide | Regulatory sunk cost; farmer skills; insurance actuarial tables assume its use |
| Monoculture | Combine-harvester fleet, contract farming structures, grain elevator logistics all assume it |
| Subsidy regime (CAP/Farm Bill) | Legislative cycle is multi-year; constituencies are concentrated (farmers) and diffuse (eaters/taxpayers) — classic concentrated-benefits/diffuse-costs politics |
| Conventional agronomist advisory | Training curricula, product-vendor kickback structures, generational career paths |
| Extractive industrial practice (the bundle) | Not a single component but a self-reinforcing system — each of the above reinforces the others |
| Commodity seed (F1) | IP/licence structures; yield advantage under high-input conditions |

The map's leftmost, bottommost quadrant (soil biology + heritage seed + agroforestry + regen agronomy) is *where Wardley's "wonder"* sits for this landscape. The rightmost, middle band (synthetic inputs + monoculture + commodity seed + CAP subsidy) is the *war/commodity* zone. The bridge between them is MRV + certification + labelling, and that bridge is being built right now.
