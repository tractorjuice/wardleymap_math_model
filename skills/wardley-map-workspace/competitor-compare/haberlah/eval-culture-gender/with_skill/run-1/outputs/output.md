# Wardley Map Analysis: Gender as a Cultural Construct (March 2022)

## Purpose

This map answers the strategic question: **Where does power over gender concentrate, and which parts of the gender system are genuinely individual and emergent versus industrialised social machinery?**

The user anchor is **the Individual** — the person whose gender is lived, expressed, and constructed — and their core needs are identity, self-expression, recognition, and belonging. Scope boundary: the full cultural construct of gender as of March 2022, from lived experience down through collective structures to the biological substrate. Time context matters: the 2021–22 window sits mid-wave in the legal and clinical formalisation of gender-identity categories in many jurisdictions, which is why Gender Identity is plotted as a component still moving rightward.

## Component Evolution Assessment

Positions are `[visibility, maturity]`. Visibility 0.97 is the user anchor at the top of the chain; 0.08 is the deepest substrate.

| Component | Stage | Position [vis, mat] | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Individual | Product | [0.97, 0.40] | User anchor. A well-understood construct in psychology, law, and liberal political theory; not a Commodity because the liberal individual is itself a contested cultural artefact. |
| Identity | Product | [0.88, 0.55] | Well-theorised (Erikson, social identity theory, narrative identity); explicit frameworks; professional measurement instruments. Users "expect to have one." |
| Lived Experience | Custom-Built | [0.82, 0.32] | Phenomenological research methods matured only in the 2010s; "lived experience" as a formal category in policy and research is still bespoke per person by definition. Signal: "We built our own account of it." |
| Self-Expression | Product | [0.80, 0.50] | Well-understood psychological construct with a mature ecosystem (fashion, social media, creative platforms) mediating it. Multiple competing schools of thought; clear vendor-equivalents. |
| Gender Expression | Product | [0.72, 0.55] | By 2022 an explicitly articulated category in APA guidance, UK Equality Act commentary, HR policy, and clinical practice; widely used and compared but not yet fully commoditised. |
| Gender Identity | Custom-Built leaning Product | [0.68, 0.42] | In active evolution in 2022: extensive academic, legal, and clinical practice but contested definitions across jurisdictions (DSM-5, ICD-11, Gender Recognition Act reviews). Boundary zone. |
| Sexual Identity | Product | [0.64, 0.57] | Established LGBTQ+ identity categories widely recognised; institutions (census, HR, health) track them using broadly consistent taxonomies. |
| Sexual Orientation | Product | [0.55, 0.65] | Codified in clinical, legal, and HR frameworks (Kinsey 1948, ICD depathologisation 1990, explicit protected-characteristic status in many jurisdictions). Reliable, well-understood, comparable. |

| Values, Rights & Beliefs | Product | [0.40, 0.63] | Extensively codified (UDHR, constitutions, ESG frameworks, corporate codes); multiple competing schools; some settled, some contested. Borderline Commodity on the rights dimension, Product on beliefs. |

| Patriarchy | Commodity (inertia) | [0.18, 0.92] | Ubiquitous global default from antiquity; deeply embedded, invisible to those inside it, catastrophic failure tolerance (entire legal and economic systems rest on it), high inertia. Passes every Commodity test. |
| Class, Property & Ownership | Commodity | [0.10, 0.90] | Industrialised machinery — class, inheritance, and property regimes are universal, highly codified in law, ubiquitous. Utility-priced in the sense that access is effectively rationed. |

The frontier of epigenetic regulation remains Custom-Built; the substrate-as-concept is Commodity. |

**Evolution arrows (12–24 month prediction from March 2022):**
- `Gender Identity`: 0.42 → 0.55 (Custom-Built → Product) as legal and clinical frameworks harden across jurisdictions.
- `Lived Experience`: 0.32 → 0.45 as phenomenological methods become standard in research and policy design.

**Inertia markers:** `Patriarchy` and `Memory, Stories & Symbols` — both show demonstrated resistance to evolution despite decades of counter-pressure.

## Key Strategic Observations

1. **The map has two clear halves with a narrow connective spine.** The top-left cluster (Lived Experience, Self-Expression, Gender Identity, Matriarchy) is individual and emergent: Custom-Built, visible, still finding its language. The bottom-right cluster (Family, Institutions, Patriarchy, Class/Property/Ownership, Memory/Stories/Symbols) is industrialised social machinery: Commodity-stage, invisible, and heavily codified. The connective spine runs through Gender Expression → Phenotype and Values, Rights & Beliefs → collective structures.

2. **Power concentrates in the bottom-right quadrant — almost exclusively.** Patriarchy, Class/Property/Ownership, and Institutions all cluster at maturity ≥ 0.88 and visibility ≤ 0.38. This is the answer to "where does power concentrate": in Commodity-stage, low-visibility structures that most inhabitants of the system cannot see because they are too ubiquitous to notice. The Patriarchy → Biological Substrate dependency encodes the way patriarchal orders have historically claimed nature as their legitimating ground.

3. **Matriarchy and Patriarchy are not symmetrical components.** Placing both honestly exposes an asymmetry of roughly two evolution stages (Custom-Built vs. Commodity) and ~0.65 on the maturity axis. This is not a modelling choice so much as an observation about the world as of March 2022: matriarchal structures exist but are rare, bespoke, and not codified into global default infrastructure.

4. **Gender Identity is the live frontier.** It sits at the boundary between Custom-Built and Product with an evolution arrow through that boundary. This is the single most strategically active component on the map — the place where legal, clinical, and institutional definitions are in active formation and contestation.

5. **Memory, Stories & Symbols is the hidden enforcement layer.** It is Commodity-stage with inertia and sits structurally between Lived Experience and Values/Rights/Beliefs. Anyone trying to change the lived layer without touching the symbolic layer is pushing against Commodity-grade infrastructure using Genesis-stage methods.

## Doctrine Check

- **Are user needs clearly identified?** Yes — Identity, Self-Expression, Gender Expression, and recognition are the top of the value chain. The map is explicitly anchored on the Individual rather than on institutions, which is itself a doctrinal stance.
- **Is there unnecessary duplication?** Sexual Identity and Sexual Orientation are adjacent but the scenario distinguishes them, and they occupy measurably different positions. Family / Institutions / Class-Property-Ownership could in principle be consolidated; they are kept separate because they carry different mechanisms of power (kinship, statutory, economic).
- **Are methods matched to evolution stages?** This map is a diagnostic rather than an operating system — but it flags a systematic mismatch at the cultural level: societal responses frequently apply Product-stage process (legal reform, HR policy) to Commodity-stage structures (Patriarchy, symbolic infrastructure). That mismatch is one reason change is slow.
- **Is there bias toward a single stage?** The map is bimodal (Custom-Built top-left, Commodity bottom-right). The middle band (Product) is relatively sparse, which is a real finding about the system, not a modelling artefact: gender's cultural constructs tend to be either live-and-unsettled or fully naturalised, with less time spent in the well-defined-multi-vendor middle.
- **Flag: >15 components.** The map has 17 components. Per the skill's guidance this should be flagged and submap splits should be offered (see Open Questions below).

## Recommended Actions

These are analytic actions the map suggests for anyone reasoning about change in this domain — not product recommendations.

1. **Target Gender Identity during its Custom-Built → Product transition.** This is the window in which institutional definitions harden. Interventions here (legal drafting, clinical guidelines, data standards) compound for decades.
2. **Treat Memory, Stories & Symbols as infrastructure, not decoration.** Work at this layer — media, education curricula, public-facing narrative — is not downstream of rights reform; it is the substrate that rights reform eventually has to rest on. The inertia marker exists for a reason.
3. **Name Patriarchy as Commodity-stage, not as a problem to be solved.** The appropriate stance toward a Commodity is not eradication in the near term but understanding the cost structure, the switching costs, and the dependencies. The map makes those visible.
4. **Use the Matriarchy / Patriarchy asymmetry as a diagnostic.** The distance between the two components on the maturity axis is a direct measure of how far the system is from symmetrical power allocation.
5. **Audit the Values, Rights & Beliefs bottleneck.** It is the single pivot between the Memory/Stories/Symbols layer and the collective structures. Most formal codification (law, policy, corporate statement) passes through it; it is a high-leverage but crowded point.

## Build vs. Buy Assessment

In a cultural-construct map, "build vs. buy" translates to **whether a component is best intervened in as a bespoke emergent practice or engaged with as a standing institution**.

| Component | Recommendation | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Lived Experience, Self-Expression, Gender Identity | Build / Invest | Custom-Built or transitional. This is where new definitions are forged; the differentiator is quality of the emergent account, not efficiency. |
| Matriarchy | Build / Study | Custom-Built. Preserving and learning from the few standing examples is the only available path. |
| Gender Expression, Sexual Identity, Sexual Orientation, Values/Rights/Beliefs | Engage / Shape | Product-stage. Institutions and standards exist; the work is comparative, policy-shaping, and compliance-adjacent. |
| Phenotype, Family, Institutions, Patriarchy, Class/Property/Ownership, Memory/Stories/Symbols, Biological Substrate | Buy / Accept-as-Given (then pressure) | Commodity-stage. Near-term change is not available; these are utility-grade substrates. The strategic move is to understand their dependencies and apply sustained pressure at specific interfaces (e.g. inheritance law, curriculum). |

## Evolution Predictions (12–24 months)

- **Gender Identity** moves further right as statutory and clinical definitions harden across major jurisdictions. Counterforce: active litigation and legislative rollback in some jurisdictions that could push it leftward again; the arrow is conditional.
- **Lived Experience** moves right as phenomenological and narrative methods become routine in policy design, clinical intake, and user research.
- **Values, Rights & Beliefs** may drift right as ESG and DEI frameworks push toward standardised corporate taxonomies, though with significant jurisdictional divergence.
- **Patriarchy** does not move. Inertia is doing exactly what inertia does.
- **Memory, Stories & Symbols** does not move at a 12–24 month scale. The substrate operates on generational time.

## Open Questions

1. **Should the map be split into submaps?** With 17 components this is above the skill's 15-component threshold. Natural submap boundaries: (a) `Biological Substrate` expanded into genes / epigenetics / environment; (b) the Power cluster (Patriarchy / Matriarchy / Class-Property-Ownership) expanded into its own map of hierarchy.
2. **Where does Exclusion sit?** The scenario names "exclusion / self-expression" as a pair. Self-Expression is on the map; Exclusion is folded into Patriarchy and Class/Property/Ownership as its structural source. A follow-up map could surface Exclusion as a first-class component to make the pair explicit.
3. **How does the map differ across jurisdictions?** March 2022 is a snapshot; the Gender Identity and Values/Rights/Beliefs positions vary materially between, for example, parts of Europe, North America, and regions with active counter-movements. A jurisdictional variant set would test the robustness of the middle band of the map.
