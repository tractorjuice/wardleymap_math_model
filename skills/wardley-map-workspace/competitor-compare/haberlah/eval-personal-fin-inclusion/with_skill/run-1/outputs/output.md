# Wardley Map Analysis: Digital Financial Inclusion — Individual in an Underbanked Region

## Purpose

This map answers the strategic question: **where is the digital financial
inclusion stack globally commoditised, and where do local landscape
constraints still dominate** — from the vantage point of an individual in an
underbanked region trying to obtain options, trade, equality, sustainability
and inclusion.

It is aimed at operators, funders, and regulators working on financial
inclusion programmes, and is intended to inform build/buy/partner decisions
across the identity, markets, banking, currency, network, and energy layers.

## Key Strategic Observations

1. **Two-layer commoditisation pattern.** The top (user needs, identity,
   trust, inclusion framing) is overwhelmingly Custom-Built or Genesis, while
   the middle-to-bottom (online payments, financial infrastructure, fiat,
   mobile, compute, internet, copper, fossil energy) is Commodity. The
   underbanked individual therefore sits above a globally commoditised stack
   they cannot fully reach, because the Custom-Built layer above it is
   missing or weak in their region.

2. **Evolution pressure concentrates in one diagonal band.** Cryptocurrency,
   CBDC, consensus algorithms, mesh networks, minigrids, and decentralised
   authority all sit in the Genesis/Custom-Built band. Their evolution
   arrows all push right simultaneously, meaning the next 24-36 months are
   a contested battleground over which of these becomes the product-stage
   default for underbanked finance.

3. **Branch banking is a high-inertia anchor that blocks evolution.**
   Branch networks are a Product with sunk-cost inertia — they soak up
   capital that could accelerate mobile-first inclusion. In underbanked
   regions this inertia is reinforced by regulation and employment
   politics, not by user demand.

4. **Copper + fossil energy are the hidden choke points.** Both are
   globally Commodity but sit on the decline curve. In underbanked regions,
   they are the only last-mile — leaving programmes exposed when the
   copper network is not maintained or when fossil supply becomes
   politically volatile.

5. **Local landscape dominates at the top and bottom; global commoditisation
   owns the middle.** Identity, trust, redistribution, mesh, minigrids are
   local. Fiat rails, mobile, compute, online payments are global. The
   strategic question for any inclusion programme is therefore: *which
   local layer do we build, and which global layer do we simply consume?*

## Doctrine Check

- **Focus on user needs** — the anchor and the five explicit needs are
  rendered, and each connects downward to enablers. Satisfied.
- **Remove duplication and bias** — the map shows both centralised and
  decentralised authority, and three currency forms, to avoid pre-committing
  to one paradigm. Satisfied, but note: the map is intentionally not
  symmetric — CBDC and crypto are less mature than fiat today.
- **Use appropriate methods** — the Genesis/Custom layer (CBDC, mesh,
  minigrids, identity, trust, decentralised authority) calls for agile,
  build-oriented methods. The Commodity layer (fiat, compute, copper,
  fossil, online payments) should be consumed, not built.
- **Single-stage bias** — no single stage dominates; the distribution is
  roughly balanced, which is healthy for a cross-layer inclusion map.
- **Challenge assumptions** — the map surfaces two often-hidden
  assumptions: (a) that branch banking is the default distribution, and
  (b) that fossil + copper will remain reliable lastmile. Both are flagged
  with inertia markers.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Buy the Commodity middle, build the Custom-Built top and bottom.**
   Consume mobile, internet, fiat rails, compute, online payments as
   utilities. Concentrate build effort on Identity, Trust, Mesh, Minigrid,
   and Redistribution — these are where landscape constraints create
   defensible local value.
2. **Pick one currency bet per corridor, not all three.** Fiat is a
   commodity floor; CBDC is genesis; crypto is a custom-built
   experiment. Operating all three in parallel fragments user experience.
   Decide per corridor based on regulatory, infrastructure, and trust
   signals.
3. **Exploit the decentralised-authority evolution arrow.** Consensus
   algorithms and decentralised authority are both moving right. Early
   operators who standardise a consensus + governance pattern for
   inclusion use cases will capture the Product-stage slot.
4. **De-risk the copper + fossil dependency.** Fund mesh and minigrid
   pilots to give banking services a second, independent lastmile. This
   also disarms the strongest inertia marker on the map.
5. **Do not try to move branch banking evolution.** It will decline
   passively with mobile penetration. Do not invest in "digitising
   branches" — invest in the inclusion primitives that route around them.

| Component | Recommendation | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Identity | Build | Custom-Built, local — no commodity form serves underbanked users today. |
| Trust | Build | Custom-Built, reputationally local; no global trust utility. |
| Decentralised Authority | Build | Custom-Built, high evolution pressure — slot is capturable. |
| CBDC | Build (co-design with regulator) | Genesis; only way to participate is to build with central bank. |
| Redistribution | Build | Custom-Built; programme design is the value. |
| Mesh Network | Build | Custom-Built; local operator advantage. |
| Minigrid Energy | Build | Custom-Built; local operator advantage, evolution pressure upward. |
| Online Payments | Outsource | Commodity; use Stripe/M-Pesa/UPI/PIX-equivalents. |
| Financial Infrastructure | Outsource | Commodity; central-bank RTGS, card networks, ACH. |
| Mobile Device | Buy | Commodity consumer good. |
| Internet Access | Buy | Commodity utility. |
| Computing Infrastructure | Buy | Commodity hyperscaler. |
| Fiat Currency | Buy | Commodity. |
| Copper Network | Buy (short term) | Commodity with inertia; buy on lowest cost, plan migration. |

## Evolution Predictions (12-24 months)

- **Cryptocurrency → Product (0.55).** Stablecoins standardise around a
  small number of issuers and regulatory regimes; corridor-level adoption
  stabilises.
- **CBDC → late Custom-Built (0.42).** More pilots become live issuance
  (beyond eNaira / Sand Dollar / e-CNY) but a globally dominant design
  pattern has not yet emerged.
- **Consensus Algorithms → Product (0.62).** PoS variants consolidate;
  PoW loses share outside Bitcoin. A small number of BFT/PoS stacks
  become de-facto defaults.
- **Satellite Network → Product (0.60).** Starlink and peers move from
  early Product to mainstream Product; latency and pricing become
  competitive for financial traffic.
- **Mesh Network → Custom-Built high (0.45).** LoRaWAN and Helium-style
  deployments mature; still not a Product, but deployment patterns
  stabilise.
- **Minigrid Energy → Custom-Built high (0.52).** Donor-plus-commercial
  hybrid models become more common; a handful of operators start to
  look like Products.
- **Decentralised Authority → Custom-Built high (0.45).** Governance
  patterns consolidate; multisig + validator models become documented
  practice.

**Inertia resisting these moves:** branch banking capex, copper lastmile,
fossil energy political economy, and — critically — user trust, which is
the slowest to migrate.

## Open Questions

1. **Which identity pattern wins for underbanked users — state-issued
   (Aadhaar/MOSIP), platform-issued (mobile-money KYC), or
   self-sovereign (DID/VC)?** The Custom-Built position of Identity
   hides a fork that the map cannot yet resolve.
2. **Does CBDC actually reach individuals, or remain wholesale?**
   Retail CBDC is plotted at Genesis; if it stalls at wholesale, the
   entire Decentralised-Authority branch absorbs more of the inclusion
   use cases.
3. **What is the minimum viable energy-and-network lastmile for
   inclusion?** Mesh + minigrid together could, in principle, substitute
   for copper + fossil — but the bundled economics for an individual
   user have not been empirically mapped.
